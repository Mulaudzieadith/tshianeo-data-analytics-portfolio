\# Unicorn Companies EDA



\## Project Overview

This project explores a dataset of unicorn companies (private companies valued at over $1 billion). The goal is to analyze how companies reach unicorn status and identify patterns across industries.



\## Business Problem

An investing firm wants insights into unicorn companies to guide future investment decisions. This analysis focuses on understanding:

\- how long it takes companies to become unicorns

\- which industries produce higher-value companies



\## Dataset

The dataset contains 1,074 unicorn companies and 10 columns, including:

\- Company

\- Valuation

\- Date Joined

\- Industry

\- City

\- Country/Region

\- Continent

\- Year Founded

\- Funding

\- Select Investors



\## Tools Used

\- Python

\- pandas

\- matplotlib

\- Jupyter Notebook



\## Key Steps

\- Loaded and explored the dataset using pandas

\- Checked data structure using `head()`, `shape`, and `info()`

\- Converted `Date Joined` to datetime format

\- Created new columns: `Year Joined` and `Years to Unicorn`

\- Sampled data for analysis

\- Cleaned the `Valuation` column to numeric format

\- Grouped data by industry

\- Created bar charts for analysis



\## Analysis Questions

\- How long does it take companies in different industries to become unicorns?

\- Which industries have the highest valuations?



\## Key Findings

\- The time it takes companies to reach unicorn status varies across industries

\- Some industries grow faster than others

\- Certain industries have significantly higher maximum valuations

\- Industry plays a major role in both growth speed and valuation potential
